Description

Dabo Kolo is a snack made out of dough. Dabo means bread in Amharic. I have tried it a few months ago. During a trip to my home country, my relatives made it at home. Dabo kolo can last a long time when sealed and unsealed. But beware, the dabo kolo will get harder when unsealed. This could be a quote from my mom:

The food is good!

Dabo Kolo Tips

    Your hand can be a bowl for the dabo kolo since each one is so small.
    Make sure the dabo kolo isn't too hard after you cook it. This is not a soft snack.
    You might be able to put a spice or spices in the dabo kolo.

Ingredients

    dough (number depends on how much you want)
    injera cooker (or any cooker like one)
    sugar (depending on how much dough you use)
    wheat powder
    1 knife (optional)

Instructions

Steps Insturctions
Step 1 Get the dough, cut them with or without the knife, and roll them until they look like thick string. Put wheat powder on the dough as you do it.
Step 2 Cut the strings of dough into ball-like shapes.
Step 3 Put the circle-like dough into the injera cooker. Cook them until they look golden brown.
Step 4 Leave the dabo kolo out for a while, with a covering, so it hardens. This might take a day or a few days.

Preparation Timing

This could range from a day or two to a few days. It will take quite some time to make.

Serving Sizes

The serving sizes depend on how much wheat you use. This can be given to lots of people in good quantites if there is enough wheat and flour (wheat powder).

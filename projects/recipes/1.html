Description

Shiro is a food that goes on a bread called injera. It has spices in it, although one might say it is not extremely spicy. I might have found it to be tasty. Shiro is a dish from Ethiopia and possibly Eritrea that combines a powdery substance and water to make a spicy-ish wot that is eaten with injera. Wot is what goes on injera. Injera is flatbread that is eaten with wot. It can vary to light brown to dark brown in color. It can be eaten on its own, or with other wot. This could be a quote from my mom:

The food is good.

Shiro Tips

    If the shiro is watery, it's probably well cooked.
    Adding salt to the shiro while heating it might add taste.
    It's probably best to keep the shiro powder in the freezer.

Ingredients

    shiro powder
    water

Instructions

Steps Instructions
Step 1 Firstly, fill the pan with water so that it is almost full.
Step 2 Secondly, heat up the pan on a stovetop on low heat.
Step 3 Thirdly, when the water is hot, put shiro powder in it.
Step 4 Fourthly, mix the shiro powder with the water while it is cooking.
Step 5 Fifthly, when it finishes cooking, it should behave mostly like a liquid. It could be a little thick. When that happens, that means you have finished making the shiro.

Preperation Timing

By guess, this could take thirty minutes to an hour.

Serving Sizes

You might be able to measure the serving sizes by water. If you use one liter of water and imagine how it could be divided with the people you want to serve, you might be able to apply that with shiro.
